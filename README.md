# Quantitative Research Portfolio

This portfolio showcases my transition from RF engineering into quantitative research through a set of projects focused on modelling, simulation, forecasting, and risk analysis. My background in RF has shaped the way I approach quantitative problems: I’m used to working with noisy systems, stochastic behaviour, and models that must be validated against real‑world data. In RF, you learn quickly that assumptions matter, edge cases matter, and reproducibility is non‑negotiable. Those same principles define strong quantitative work.

I’ve taken the engineering mindset I developed—modular design, statistical intuition, automation, and rigorous validation—and applied it directly to financial modelling. The projects in this portfolio reflect that shift. They demonstrate my ability to build pricing engines, risk models, volatility frameworks, factor decompositions, and systematic strategies with the same discipline I applied in RF measurement and simulation.

My strengths align naturally with **Market Risk** and **Model Validation** roles:  
I understand how to test models, challenge assumptions, analyse distributional behaviour, and build frameworks that are transparent, explainable, and grounded in statistical reasoning. I enjoy breaking down complex systems into components that can be validated independently and then integrated into a coherent whole. This portfolio is the practical evidence of that capability.

## Projects

- **nvidia-stock-forecasting-ml** — End‑to‑end ML forecasting pipeline with feature engineering, model comparison, and predictive uncertainty analysis.
- **nvidia-ml-risk-modelling** — ML‑based risk modelling including volatility estimation, scenario analysis, and stress‑style behaviour evaluation.
- **systematic-trading-backtester** — A modular backtesting engine with PnL decomposition, position sizing, and risk‑adjusted performance metrics.
- **quant-pricing-lab** — Pricing models including Black‑Scholes, binomial trees, stochastic processes, and Greeks for model validation workflows.
- **garch-volatility-modelling-framework** — GARCH‑family volatility modelling for forecasting, risk estimation, and distribution diagnostics.
- **pca-factor-risk-engine** — Factor decomposition using PCA to identify systematic risk drivers and validate factor structures.
- **clt-in-action** — Simulation of the Central Limit Theorem and its implications for aggregation, convergence, and risk estimation.
- **financial-time-series-risk-modelling** — Analysis of returns, autocorrelation, volatility clustering, and distributional assumptions in financial time series.
- **gaussian-vs-fat-tailed-distributions** — Comparative study of distributional behaviour, highlighting tail risk and model misspecification.
- **law-of-large-numbers-simulation** — LLN simulations demonstrating convergence properties relevant to expectation‑based risk metrics.
- **random-walk-price-simulator** — Baseline stochastic price generator for understanding drift, variance, and path‑dependent behaviour.
- **Stochastic-Price-Simulator-with-Multiple-Noise-...** — Multi‑noise‑source stochastic simulator showing how different noise processes affect price evolution.
- **time-series-arima-dax** — ARIMA modelling on DAX data with diagnostics, forecasting, and residual analysis for model validation.
